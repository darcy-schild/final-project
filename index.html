Recent findings & trends

In 2017, U.S. imports of women’s elastic knit (yoga) pants surpassed those of jeans.

Source: US Census Bureau

You may be thinking that yoga pants are just in higher demand because they're simply a comfortable alternative to jeans or other wardrobe options.

One thing's for sure, though: It is true that people in the U.S. are increasingly picking up the practice of yoga.

According to reports from IBISWorld, over the next five years, revenue of the yoga and pilates industry is expected to grow by 4.4%, "which can [partially] be attributed to the industry’s robust ancillary product sales, such as yoga clothing."

Read on to explore more about yoga in the United States and why many Americans are jumping on the bandwagon, not just for the comfy pants — but also for the physical and mental health benefits.

Data: Yoga usage in 2012 vs. 2017


Why is yoga becoming more popular? According to the 2017 National Health Survey from the National Institutes of Health, a large proportion of U.S. adults who practiced yoga perceived a wide array of health benefits, including:

Improved sleep
59 percent of U.S. adult respondents said that practicing yoga improved their sleep.
Motivation to eat healthier
63 percent said that practicing yoga motivated them to eat healthier.
Reduced stress
86 percent of US. adult respondents said that practicing yoga reduced stress.

Vinyasa yoga

Vinyasa yoga is a "flowing, dynamic form of yoga." It takes a "step-by-step approach that systematically and appropriately takes a student from one point and safely lands them at the next point," focusing on deep, slow breathing.

Fitness yoga

This style of yoga "primarily emphasizes the physical dimensions of yoga practice. Typical objectives might include aerobic conditioning, gaining strength, or building stamina."
